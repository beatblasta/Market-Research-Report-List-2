<p><ul><li>Double Layer</li><li>Pseudocapacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Symmetric supercapacitors can be divided into two main types: double layer and pseudocapacitor. Double layer supercapacitors store energy through the adsorption of ions at the electrode/electrolyte interface, resulting in a high surface area to volume ratio. Pseudocapacitors store energy through fast and reversible redox reactions at the electrode surface, allowing for higher energy density compared to double layer capacitors. Both types of symmetric supercapacitors have their own advantages and applications in various industries.</p></p>
