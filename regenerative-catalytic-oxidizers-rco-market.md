<p><p>Regenerative Catalytic Oxidizers (RCO) are advanced air pollution control systems that use a combination of catalytic oxidation and heat recovery to effectively treat volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and hazardous air pollutants (HAPs) emitted from industrial processes. RCO systems work by passing polluted air through a catalytic bed where VOCs are oxidized into carbon dioxide and water vapor. The heat generated during the process is captured and used to preheat the incoming process air, making RCO systems highly energy efficient.</p><p>The Regenerative Catalytic Oxidizers (RCO) Market is witnessing steady growth due to increasing environmental regulations regarding air pollution control, particularly in industries such as automotive, chemical, and food processing. <p><p>Regenerative Catalytic Oxidizers (RCO) are commonly found in two main types in the market - 3-Chamber RCO and Rotary RCO. 3-Chamber RCO systems consist of three chambers where the exhaust gases are directed through a series of heat exchangers and catalyst beds to efficiently remove pollutants. On the other hand, Rotary RCO systems use a rotating heat exchanger to transfer heat and facilitate the oxidation process. Both types of RCOs are effective in reducing harmful emissions and are commonly used in industrial applications.</p></p>
