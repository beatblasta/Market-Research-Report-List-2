<p><p>Industrial acetic acid is produced through various methods, including carbonylation of methanol, hydrocarbon oxidation, paraffin oxidation, oxidation of olefins, and other markets. Carbonylation of methanol involves reacting methanol with carbon monoxide under high pressure and temperature. Hydrocarbon oxidation uses hydrocarbons as raw materials and oxidizes them with oxygen. Paraffin oxidation involves the oxidation of paraffin wax with acetic acid as a catalyst. Oxidation of olefins uses olefins as starting materials and oxidizes them to form acetic acid. Other markets include various processes such as fermentative production or direct synthesis.</p></p>
