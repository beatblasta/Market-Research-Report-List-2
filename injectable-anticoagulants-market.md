<p><p>Injectable anticoagulants are medications used to prevent blood clot formation and reduce the risk of stroke, heart attack, and other serious conditions. The main types of injectable anticoagulants include Low Molecular Weight Heparins (LMWH), Unfractionated Heparin, Fibrinolytics, and others. LMWHs are commonly used for long-term anticoagulation therapy, while Unfractionated Heparin is often used in emergency situations. Fibrinolytics are used to break down blood clots in emergency situations like heart attacks. Other types of injectable anticoagulants may include Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs) and Warfarin.</p></p>
