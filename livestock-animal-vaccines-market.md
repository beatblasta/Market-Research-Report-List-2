<p><p>Livestock animal vaccines can be categorized into three main types: live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and other vaccines. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of the virus or bacteria, stimulating the immune system without causing disease. Inactivated vaccines use killed versions of the pathogen to trigger an immune response. Other vaccines include recombinant vaccines, subunit vaccines, and DNA vaccines, which rely on different methods to protect animals against diseases. Each type of vaccine plays a crucial role in maintaining the health and productivity of livestock animals.</p></p>
