<p><p>Mechanical sewing machines can be categorized into two main types based on their weight and capacity: light-duty and heavy-duty. Light-duty machines are designed for basic sewing tasks such as mending, hemming, and simple garment construction. They are compact, portable, and suitable for light fabrics. On the other hand, heavy-duty machines are built for heavy fabrics, thick materials, and frequent use. They have stronger motors, sturdier frames, and enhanced durability to handle more challenging sewing projects with ease.</p></p>
