<p><p>Copper oxide nanoparticles are categorized into four main types based on their sizes: Below 20nm, 20-50nm, 50-100nm, and Above 100nm market. Nanoparticles below 20nm are highly reactive and have larger surface areas, making them ideal for various applications such as catalysis and electronics. Those between 20-50nm are commonly used in biomedical and environmental fields due to their balance of reactivity and stability. Particles between 50-100nm are suitable for antimicrobial coatings and water treatment. Nanoparticles above 100nm are mainly used in optical and sensor applications.</p></p>
