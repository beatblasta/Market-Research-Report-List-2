<p><p>Active Fire Protection Systems refer to systems that require some form of action to suppress or control a fire, such as fire sprinkler systems, fire suppression systems, and fire detection systems. On the other hand, Passive Fire Protection Systems are designed to prevent the spread of fire by containing it within a specific area, such as fire-resistant doors, fire-resistant walls, and fire barriers. Both types of systems play a crucial role in ensuring the safety of occupants and minimizing property damage in the event of a fire.</p></p>
