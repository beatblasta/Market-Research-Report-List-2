<p><p>The metal alloy current sensing resistor market can be categorized into two main types: Through Hole and Surface Mount Device (SMD). Through Hole resistors require leads to pass through holes in a circuit board for installation, providing robust mechanical support. Meanwhile, SMD resistors are soldered directly onto the surface of the circuit board, offering space-saving and automated assembly advantages. Both types cater to different preferences and requirements in the market, with Through Hole resistors being more traditional and SMD resistors being more modern and compact.</p></p>
